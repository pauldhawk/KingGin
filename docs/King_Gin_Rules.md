# KING GIN

*Card Game Rules*

## Overview

King Gin is a contract rummy-style card game for 3 or more players. Each round, players are dealt 11 cards and must complete a specific combination of sets and runs (the "contract"). There are 7 rounds, and the contracts become more demanding as the game progresses. The player with the lowest cumulative score after all 7 rounds wins.

## Setup

- 2 standard decks of cards shuffled together, including Jokers.
- Wild cards: Jokers and 2s.
- Deal 11 cards to each player every round.
- Place the remaining cards face down as the draw (stock) pile.
- Flip the top card face up next to the draw pile to start the discard pile.
- The player to the dealer's left takes the first turn. The deal rotates one seat to the left each round.

## Sets & Runs

**Group (Set):** 3 or more cards of the same rank (e.g. three Kings, four 4s). Suit does not matter. Wild cards can substitute for any card.

**Sequence (Run):** 4 or more cards of the same suit in consecutive rank (e.g. 5♥, 6♥, 7♥, 8♥). Aces may be played high or low within a sequence, and sequences may wrap around the corner (e.g. Q-K-A-2 is valid). Wild cards can fill gaps in a run or extend it at either end.

**Wilds in melds:** A group or sequence may contain any number of wild cards. There is no cap on how many wilds a meld may include.

## The 7 Rounds

Each round has a required contract — the combination of groups and sequences a player must complete to go out. 11 cards are dealt every round. The contract size grows each round, so the final round (Three Sequences of 4) requires 12 cards and therefore requires at least one May I to draw the extra card needed.

| Round | Cards Dealt | Contract | Notes |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------|
| 1 | 11 | Two Groups of 3 (6 card contract) | |
| 2 | 11 | One Group of 3 and One Sequence of 4 (7 card contract) | |
| 3 | 11 | Two Sequences of 4 (8 card contract) | |
| 4 | 11 | Three Groups of 3 (9 card contract) | |
| 5 | 11 | Two Groups of 3 and One Sequence of 4 (10 card contract) | |
| 6 | 11 | One Group of 3 and Two Sequences of 4 (11 card contract) | |
| 7 | 11 | Three Sequences of 4 (12 card contract) | Requires at least 1 May I to reach the 12 cards needed. |

## Gameplay

### On Your Turn

1. **Draw one card** — either the top card of the draw pile or the top card of the discard pile. Before drawing from the draw pile, you must give other players the opportunity to call "May I?" on the current top discard (see May I below).
2. **Discard one card** from your hand face up on top of the discard pile to end your turn.

### Going Out

You go out when every card remaining in your hand forms part of your complete contract for the round. You lay your full contract down face up and discard your final card face down on the discard pile. The round ends immediately.

You may only lay down your contract when you are going out. You cannot lay a partial contract on earlier turns, and you cannot add cards to another player's melds.

### May I?

Each player may take the top discard out of turn at most 3 times per round. Use pennies, chips, or other tokens to track how many May I's each player has used.

- When it is not your turn, you may call "May I?" to claim the top card of the discard pile.
- You may only take the discard via May I if the player whose turn it is does not want it. If the active player says no, they must then draw from the draw pile — they cannot change their mind and take the discard after declining.
- If more than one player calls May I on the same discard and the active player allows it, the player closest to the active player (going clockwise from the active player) gets priority.
- A player who takes a May I also draws one additional card from the top of the draw pile. They do not take a turn — play reverts to the active player, who then completes their own turn (drawing and discarding normally).
- Round 7 requires at least 1 May I to complete the contract, because three sequences of 4 require 12 cards and only 11 are dealt. The 3-per-round maximum still applies.

## Scoring

After a player goes out, each remaining player counts the point value of the cards left in their hand.

**Partial contracts count:** if a player has the combinations needed for their contract (or part of it) in hand, the cards that would have been part of their contract are not scored — only the cards that cannot be part of the contract are counted against them.

| Card Type | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Number cards (3–6, 8–10) | 5 points |
| Face cards (J, Q, K) | 10 points |
| Aces | 15 points |
| 2s (wild) | 20 points |
| Jokers | 20 points |

The player who goes out scores 0 for that round. Scores are tallied after each round.

## Winning the Game

After all 7 rounds are played, the player with the lowest cumulative score wins.

*Lowest score wins. Good luck!*
